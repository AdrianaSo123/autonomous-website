Ever since my mom died, I cry in H-Mart. H-Mart is a supermarket chain that specializes in Asian food. The H stands for Hanadu.

H-Mart is more than just a store to me; it's a connection to my heritage and memories of my mother. Every aisle holds familiar sights, smells, and sounds that remind me of times spent with her. The vibrant colors of fresh produce, the comforting aroma of simmering soups, and the lively chatter in various Asian languages create a tapestry of nostalgia.

In this place, I find a unique solace. It's a space where I can openly grieve amidst the familiarity of cultural symbols and foods that my mom loved. Each visit is a bittersweet journey through our shared past, where the act of shopping becomes a quiet conversation with her memory.

H-Mart is not just a supermarket; it is a sanctuary for those, like me, who find comfort in the embrace of cultural familiarity while navigating the complexities of loss.
