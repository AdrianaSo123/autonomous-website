In the ongoing discourse about AI policy, we often encounter two polarized viewpoints. On one hand, there are advocates for stringent regulation, emphasizing the potential risks AI poses. On the other hand, there are proponents of minimal regulation, warning that overregulation could stifle innovation, while claiming that the risks are overstated.

However, the dilemma is not simply a matter of slamming on the brakes or hitting the gas. Technologists sometimes convey the notion that unless you are deeply involved in the technical intricacies, your opinion on AI's future is unwarranted. While expertise is undeniably valuable, history reminds us that those impacted by new technologies must have a hand in shaping their use.

It is crucial not to be daunted by the technology itself or by the entities developing it. You do not need to be a scientist or an engineer to contribute meaningfully to the conversation. Everyone's voice matters when it comes to deciding how AI should be integrated into society. By fostering a more inclusive dialogue, we can navigate the complexities of AI regulation and innovation more effectively.
